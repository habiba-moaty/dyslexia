What can Parents do?

Parents can play a crucial role in helping their child succeed by taking the following steps:

  • Address any concerns early on. If you suspect that your child may have dyslexia, speak with your child's healthcare provider as soon as possible. Early intervention can increase the chances of success.
  • Read aloud with your child. Starting when your child is young is ideal, but it's never too late to begin. Introducing books as a toy for babies can promote fun, learning, and social interaction with caregivers. Read stories to your child and try listening to recorded books with them. When your child is old enough, read stories together after they've heard them.
  • Collaborate with your child's school. Discuss how the school will support your child in succeeding with the teacher. You are your child's best advocate.
  • Encourage reading time. Dedicate time every day to read with your child. To improve reading skills, children must practice reading. Encourage your child to read as their skills develop, and have them read aloud to you.
  • Set a daily time for yourself to read something while your child is reading - this sets an example and supports your child. Demonstrate that reading can be enjoyable.
