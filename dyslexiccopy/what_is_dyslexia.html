What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a common learning disability that primarily affects reading, writing, and spelling. Given that it is a particular learning disability, it affects specific learning skills, like reading and writing.

Any dyslexic child's life can be transformed by understanding how to spot, encourage, and strengthen dyslexic thinking.

The collection of symptoms and syndromes has been divided into three sections: oral, reading, and writing. Remember that some of these symptoms may be present in an average kid. This does not imply that the individual is dyslexic. A dyslexia patient will show several or most of these symptoms.

Dealing with dyslexia is undoubtedly a difficult experience. Not only for your child but also for yourself. However, dyslexia is not uncommon. Approximately 70-80% of children exhibit some signs of dyslexia at the start of learning to read. The symptoms begin to fade with the passing of time.
